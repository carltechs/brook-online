Beyond the Bones

What's Really in the Skeletal System?

"When people think of the skeletal system, they usually imagine a model skeleton standing in a science classroom. But there’s a lot more to it than just bones! Your skeletal system is a complex structure made up of different parts that work together to support, protect, and move your body."

Here are the main components:

01

Bones

These are the framework of your body, giving it shape and support. Adults typically have between 206 and 213 bones.

02

Muscles

You have over 600 muscles that help you move, breathe, and even digest food. They’re made of stretchy, woven fibers.

03

Cartilage

This flexible, rubbery tissue cushions your joints and keeps bones from grinding against each other.

04

Ligaments

These are strong bands of tissue that connect bones to other bones, helping stabilize your joints.

05

Tendons

These tough cords attach muscles to bones, allowing your muscles to move your skeleton

06

Joints

A joint is where two bones meet. It includes some or all of the parts above and allows for movement in different directions.
