Main Functions (Jobs) of the Skeletal System

What are the functions of the skeletal system?

"The skeletal system plays many roles in the body—some are more obvious than others, but each one is essential for overall health. Its main responsibilities include:"

01

Shaping your body

Your skeleton holds up your body’s weight and provides a structure for muscles and other tissues to attach to.

02

Movement support

Your muscles, joints, and connective tissues work together with your skeleton to allow your body to move smoothly and efficiently.

03

Organ protection

Your bones act like internal armor, shielding vital organs. For example, the skull guards your brain, your ribcage protects your heart and lungs, and your spine surrounds your spinal cord.

04

Blood cell production

The soft marrow inside certain bones produces essential blood cells, including red and white cells that help keep your body functioning properly.

05

Mineral storage

Bones serve as storage centers for crucial minerals such as calcium and vitamin D, helping to maintain the body’s mineral balance.
